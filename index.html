Overview

Oceans and seas are essential for the Earth. They cover most of the planet, hold most of its water, and support over three billion jobs. They provide food, improve health, regulate the climate, absorb greenhouse gases, and supply water and oxygen. Oceans are also rich in marine life.

What is an Ocean?

Threats to Oceans

    Overfishing
  • Overfishing is the practice of catching fish at a rate faster than they can reproduce, leading to the depletion of fish populations and disruption of marine ecosystems.

    • Pollution
  • Pollution from plastic and chemical waste harms marine life, disrupts ecosystems, and threatens human health by contaminating oceans and waterways.

    • Climate change and ocean acidification
  • Climate change and ocean acidification are threatening marine ecosystems by raising ocean temperatures and increasing the acidity of seawater, which harms marine species and disrupts food chains.

    • Loss of biodiversity
  • The loss of biodiversity threatens ecosystems' stability and resilience, leading to the extinction of species and the collapse of vital environmental services.

Solutions and Actions

There are various actions being taken globally to protect our oceans, such as:

    Establishing marine protected areas
  • Establishing marine protected areas can help conserve biodiversity, restore fish populations, and protect vulnerable ecosystems by limiting human activities and promoting sustainable practices.

    • Promoting sustainable fishing practices
  • Promoting sustainable fishing practices helps preserve fish populations, protect marine ecosystems, and ensure long-term food security by reducing overfishing and minimizing environmental damage.

    • Reducing plastic waste through recycling and waste management
  • Reducing plastic waste through recycling and effective waste management helps prevent pollution, conserve resources, and protect marine and terrestrial ecosystems from harmful plastic accumulation.

    • Raising awareness about ocean conservation
  • Raising awareness about ocean conservation inspires action to protect marine ecosystems and preserve the oceans for future generations.

How You Can Get Involved

You can contribute by:

    Reducing single-use plastics
  • Reducing single-use plastics helps decrease pollution, protect marine life, and conserve natural resources by minimizing waste and encouraging sustainable alternatives.

    • Supporting sustainable seafood choices
  • Supporting sustainable seafood choices helps protect fish populations, reduce environmental impact, and promote healthier oceans.

    • Participating in local beach clean-ups
  • Participating in local beach clean-ups helps reduce pollution, protect marine life, and keep coastal ecosystems healthy.

    • Use Social Media
  • Spread awareness about marine conservation efforts, campaigns, and success stories.
